====================================================================
BIZIMI ESCROW & RESOLUTION POLICY OPTIONS
====================================================================

Below are the 4 major edge-cases for the Escrow system and the proposed 
solutions. Please review them and let me know your decisions so they 
can be implemented.

--------------------------------------------------------------------
SCENARIO 1: Client Ghosting (Post-Submission)
The freelancer submits the work, but the client never logs back in 
to approve or request changes.
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Option A (Auto-Release - Upwork Style): 
If the client doesn't respond within 5 days, the system automatically 
marks it as "Approved" and releases the funds to the freelancer.

Option B (Auto-Escalate to Admin - Safer for new platforms): 
After 5 days of silence, the system automatically creates a "Client 
Abandonment" Dispute. An Admin looks at the work, compares it to the 
scope, and manually releases or refunds.

*Recommendation: Option B (Safer while Bizimi is growing)*


--------------------------------------------------------------------
SCENARIO 2: Freelancer Ghosting (Post-Funding, Pre-Submission)
The client funds the job, but the freelancer disappears and never 
submits any work, missing the deadline.
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Option A (Client-Triggered Refund): 
If the deadline passes, a "Cancel & Refund" button appears for the 
client. If clicked, the freelancer has 48 hours to object. If no 
objection, the money returns to the client.

Option B (Strict Deadline Auto-Refund): 
If the job duration was "2 weeks", and 3 weeks pass with absolutely 
no submission in the Workspace, the system automatically refunds the 
client and penalizes the freelancer.

*Recommendation: Option A (Allows for informal deadline extensions)*


--------------------------------------------------------------------
SCENARIO 3: The "Infinite Revision" Loop
The freelancer submits work. The client clicks "Request Changes". 
This repeats infinitely. The freelancer is trapped; the money is stuck.
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Option A (Revision Caps): 
Hardcode a limit (e.g., max 3 revision requests). After the 3rd 
request, the client can only "Approve" or "Open Dispute".

Option B (Time-Based Dispute Lock): 
No limit on revisions, but after 14 days of being in the "Changes 
Requested" loop, either party can forcefully click "Escalate to Admin" 
to break the stalemate.

*Recommendation: Option B (More flexible for complex projects)*


--------------------------------------------------------------------
SCENARIO 4: Mutual Cancellation (No-Fault)
Halfway through, both parties realize the project must stop (e.g. 
technical limitations). Neither is angry, they just want to cancel.
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Option A (Manual Dispute): 
They have to open a Dispute, write "We agreed to stop", and wait 
for an Admin to read it and refund the client.

Option B (Mutual Cancel Button): 
An "End Contract" button exists. If the client clicks it, they can 
propose a split (e.g., "Take 20% for your time, refund me 80%"). If 
the freelancer clicks "Accept", the money moves instantly without 
Admin intervention.

*Recommendation: Option B (Saves Admins from unnecessary support tickets)*
